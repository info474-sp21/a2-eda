Exploratory Data Analysis

The Dataset

The dataset I have selected is the Mental Health in Tech dataset from Kaggle. The dataset contains information about respondents, their employers, and as well as their answers to a survey on mental health in the tech workplace. I hope to use this dataset to try to understand the attitudes towards mental health in the tech workplace.

Questions

  1. How does mental health interfere with people's work by age?
  2. Does age play a role in whether people seek treatment for a mental health condition?
  3. How do attitudes towards mental health differ from physical health?
  4. Do employers provide employees with enough resources regarding mental health?
  5. Are people more likely to discuss a mental health issue with their coworkers or supervisors?

Exploratory Visual Analysis

Phase 1

The dataset contains 27 variables and 1,259 rows (records). Of the 27 variables, 22 of them are nominal/categorical. Because a majority of my variables are categorical/nominal, most of my viusalizations will bar plots rather than scatter plots or line plots. One variable, Timestamp, is temporal. The Age and number of employees (no_employees) are the only two numerical variables. Two variables, Country and state, are geographical. The main variables I will be using to answer my questions are:

  • Age
  • Country
  • treatment (Respondent sought treatment for mental health condition?)
  • no_employees (number of employees at organization)
  • benefits (employer provides mental health benefits?)
  • seek_help (employer provides resources to learn moer about mental health issues and how to seek help?)
  • leave (how easy is it for you to take medical leave for a mental health condition)
  • coworkers (Would respondent be willing to discuss a mental health issue with their coworkers?)
  • supervisor (Would you be willing to discuss a mental health issue with their supervisor?)
  • mentalvsphysical (Does respondent feel that their employer takes mental health as seriously as physical health?)

How are variables distributed?

Visualization 1 - Distribution of Age

From the visualization, we can see that in the dataset, there is one record with someone who has an age of almost 100,000,000,000. This is not possible, so I will need to filter out ages that are greater than 100.


Visualization 2 - Distribution of Age after filtering out ages over 100

From the visualization, we can see that in the dataset, there multiple records of people who have negative ages. This is also not possible, so I will need to filter out ages that are less than 0.


Visualization 3 - Distribution of Age after filtering out ages over 100 and ages less than 0

From the visualization, we can see that there is a bell curve shape, which indicates a relatively normal distribution of ages. The majority of respondents were between 20 to 40 years old, with 29 year olds being the most frequent respondent.

Visualization 4 - Distribution of Work Interference

From the visualization, we see that there is an 'NA' response category, meaning that people did not have a mental health condition. To view the distribution for work interference of a mental health condition, I will need to filter out the 'NA' responses.

Visualization 5 - Distribution of Work Interference after filtering out NA responses

From the visualization, we see that the most frequent response is that a mental health condition sometimes interferes with their work. Although the rest of the response categories are about half the frequency or less than the Sometimes catetgory, the frequency is somewhat similar among the rest of the options for Never, Rarely, and NA, with Often being the least frequent.
